WELCOME TO KURUNEGALA DISTRICT

Kurunegala is a district in the North Western Province of Sri Lanka. The land area is 4816 square kilometers. The total population of the district was 1,610,299.
We welcome you to find your unique Kurunegala district travel experience.

Samadhi Buddha Statue

The world's tallest stone Samadhi Buddha statue is located in Kurunegala.

Ethgala

Athagala is one of the seven neglected rocks in the city of Sri Lanka. The rocky face is located 316 meters from the city and is shaped like an elephant, which explains the name of the rock.

Ridi Viharaya

Ridi Viharaya is a 2nd century BC Theravada Buddhist temple located in the village of Ridigama in Sri Lanka. A valuable feature or product here is the ivory door. Its top is decorated with carvings called "Panchanarighataya". The Buddha statue here is twelve cubits high. In the middle of it is a dragon pandal. The dagoba is located on a rock. Below the rock is the Maha Vihara. The keys attached to the gates of this temple are those used in the days of the Sinhala kings. You can see the murals in the upper temple.
