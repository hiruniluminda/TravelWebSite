WELCOME TO GALLE DISTRICT

Galle District is a district in Southern Province, Sri Lanka. It is one of 25 districts of Sri Lanka, the second level administrative division of the country.
Galle District is located on the southeastern coast of Sri Lanka. It has an area of 1,652 kmÂ².Galle is the provincial capital and largest city of Southern Province, Sri Lanka and is the capital of Galle district.Other prominent towns include Ahangama, Ambalangoda, Balapitiya, Koggala and etc.
Galle District's population was 1,130,000 in 2019. The majority of the population are Sinhalese, with a minority Sri Lankan muslims and Tamil population. We welcome you to find your unique Galle district travel experience.

Galle Dutch Fort

Built by the Portuguese during their conquests in the 16th century, the fort was later fortified and conquered by the Dutch in the 17th century and later by the British. Today, the fort has been transformed into a place of history, romance and beauty, attracting tourists.
Fifty Lighthouse Street is located in the fort and provides a perfect base for you to explore the beauty of Galle. You can walk along the wall and see the magnificent structures such as the Galle Lighthouse, the Galle Clock Tower and the Groot Kirk.

The National Museum of Galle

The Galle National Museum is housed in the oldest Dutch building in Galle Fort, built in 1656. The Galle National Museum exhibits a number of archeological and anthropological objects inherited from the Southern Province. A collection of traditional masks used for various rituals, a collection of ornaments made of turtle shells, antique wood carvings and a collection of beers
Important visitors to the museum are fascinated exhibits. Among the exhibits are porcelain objects and weapons used by Dutch soldiers. The Galle National Museum plays an important role as an educational center in the Southern Province.

Jungle Beach

Jungle Beach, one of the most secluded beaches on the south coast, is a beautiful stretch of golden sand that allows you to appreciate the majesty of Mount Rumassala on the one hand and the majestic Indian Ocean on the other.

Japanese Peace Pagoda

Unawatuna is hidden among the trees in the rainforests but the "Peace Pagoda" overlooking the water from the historic Galle Fort in the Bay of Galle is one of the four Japanese peace pagodas found in Sri Lanka. Located on Rummasala Hill, it was built in 2005 with the help of Japanese Buddhist monks as part of a plan to build temples of peace in conflict zones.

Rumassala Sanctuary

Rumassala Sanctuary is a popular tourist destination located on Jungle Beach Road, Unawatuna, Galle. This sanctuary is a botanical paradise with rare plants and herbs as well as endemic birds.
Visit to Rumassala Sanctuary: From Galle pass Deva along Matara Road to Unawatuna and reach Rumassala Sanctuary.

Maritime Museum

This is located in the old Dutch warehouse in Galle Fort. Species of submarines used for passenger transport and maritime trade in southern Sri Lanka, traditional lifestyles of the fishing community and similar fisheries techniques and replicas, marine ecosystems and their flora and fauna, coral reefs and specimens of some invertebrates Can be done and a large whale skeleton mounted on the roof gives you a wonderful experience.

Mahamodara Sea Turtle Hatchery Centre

Mahamodara Sea Turtle Breeding Station, located a few kilometers from Galle Fort, is an active forest conservation project aimed at conserving rare and exotic turtle species in Sri Lanka. Hunting, pollution, and many other factors make it difficult for turtles to breed and lay their eggs under safe conditions, making them endangered.

Galle Harbour

Galle Harbor is a natural harbor located on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka. Today, the Port of Galle serves as one of the most active regional ports in the country. It is also the only Sri Lankan port that caters to recreational vessels. International Harbor Associations have identified the Port of Galle as one of the world's top tourist attractions.

Yatagala Raja Maha Viharaya

When Buddhism was first established in the island, the Yatagala Raja Maha Viharaya was one of the few monuments in the Galle District that illustrates the history of Sri Lanka. This temple has several intricate works of art that originated from different kingdoms and shows how religion is deeply intertwined with the way of life.

Koggala Beach

Koggala Beach is a scenic beach located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka. Koggala is the perfect place to relax, surrounded by jungle vegetation and blue water on golden sands. If you are an active adventurer, you can find some of the best surfing opportunities in the country and also take personal surfing lessons if you want. There is also a turtle breeding ground where you can see beautiful marine life.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna is one of the largest tourist destinations in Sri Lanka and is one of the most 'famous' beaches in the country. It is a beautiful banana-shaped beach with golden sands and turquoise waters surrounded by green palm trees.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a 11250 hectare National Park in the Rakwana Range in the southwestern part of the Central Highlands of Sri Lanka at an elevation of 300-1170 m. Part of the lowland rainforest ecosystem of Sri Lanka, this unripe mountain rainforest was protected from commercialization due to its inaccessibility. It is a safe haven for endemic species of plants as well as insects, amphibians, reptiles, birds and mammals.
