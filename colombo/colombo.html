WELCOME TO COLOMBO DISTRICT

Covering an area of 642 km2, Colombo District is the smallest district in Sri Lanka. It is located in the western part of the island's west coast. The commercial capital of Sri Lanka is Colombo and the political capital of Sri Jayewardenepura Kotte is located in this district. It has a population of 2,235,000 and has the highest population density of any district in Sri Lanka.
Other prominent towns include Kasbawa, Piliyandala, Nugegoda, Homagama and etc.
We welcome you to find your unique Colombo district travel experience.

Mount Lavinia Beach

Mount Lavinia beach is located outside the city of Colombo. There are many nice restaurant pubs along the beach as well as relaxing areas. Mount Lavinia beach is a must visit place for any tourist visiting Colombo. The weekend can be crowded with locals, but during the week it is relaxing and peaceful.

Viharamahadevi Park

Viharamahadevi Park is a public park in Colombo, adjacent to the National Museum of Sri Lanka. It is the oldest and largest park in the Port of Colombo. After the war, the park was renovated in 1951 and opened to the public.

Galle Face Green

Galle Face is a half kilometer long strip of land that connects to the beach in Colombo, the capital of Sri Lanka. In 1859 the belt was built for horseback riding and golfing.

National zoological Garden In Sri Lanka

Dehiwala National Zoo is a zoo located in Sri Lanka. Located in Dehiwala town. There are about 350 species of animals here and about 3000 animals.

Crow Island Beach Park

Crow Island Beach Park is a family friendly amusement park located in Colombo 15. Parks boast of mangroves, swamps and coastal vegetation. With many restaurants, walkways and bike tracks, it's a great place to relax, walk and have fun.

Gangaramaya Park

Gangaramaya Park is a children's park with lots of greenery and plants. It is a great place to relax or even watch the birds as they are frequented by rare birds.This is one of the must-see gardens in Sri Lanka.

Kelaaniya Raja Maha Viharaya

Kelaniya Rajamaha Viharaya is a Buddhist temple located in the Kelaniya area near the Kelani River in the Gampaha Administrative District of Sri Lanka. According to the belief of the Buddhists, Kelaniya is a sacred place which was purified by the third and last visit of Gautama Buddha to Sri Lanka. The third visit of the Supreme Buddha to Ceylon took place 8 years after attaining Enlightenment. Thus, the history of the Kelaniya Sacred Area dates back to BC. It can be considered as far as 500 km.

Beira Lake

Beira Lake is located in the city of Sri Lanka. It is almost completely surrounded by elements of the city. In the past it was spread over an area of ​​165 hectares but today it has been reduced to 65 hectares due to various reasons.

Gangaramaya Temple

Gangaramaya Viharaya is one of the most important temples in Colombo, Sri Lanka. With a mix of modern architecture and cultural essence. Located on Lake Beira. It was completed in the late 19th century.

National Museum Colombo

The Colombo National Museum, the largest museum in Sri Lanka, was established in 1877. The museum is home to a number of important collections, including the crown and throne of the Kandyan kings. Situated in a two-storey building, the museum is adorned with large cassava trees and lush green grass in front. Upon entering the museum, a fourth-century Buddha can also be seen. You can also find antique art, carvings and statues with a collection of antique ghost masks, as well as guns, swords and more.

Independence Square

In 1948, it was decided to construct an Independence Monument to commemorate the long and glorious history of Sri Lanka, marking the triumphant victory of Sri Lanka in independence. Accordingly, a building similar to the Kandy wedding hall was designed. The walls are lined with paintings depicting various events. So the whole building is designed as a reflection of the glorious ancient culture in the fullest sense of the word.

Colombo Fort

Fort is the hub of business in Colombo, the commercial capital of Sri Lanka. The fort was first built by the Portuguese and later used by the Dutch and the English.
