WELCOME TO MATARA DISTRICT

Matara District is a district in Southern Province, Sri Lanka. It is one of 25 districts of Sri Lanka, the second level administrative division of the country.
Matara District is located on the southeastern coast of Sri Lanka. It has an area of 1,283 kmÂ². The district capital is Matara town.Other prominent towns include Weligama, Mirissa, Kaburupitiya, Devinuwara and etc.
Matara District's population was 803,999 in 2012. The majority of the population are Sinhalese, with a minority Sri Lankan Moor and Indian Tamil population. We welcome you to find your unique Matara district travel experience.

Polhena Beach

Polhena Beach is located in the city of Matara on the southern coast of Sri Lanka. It is one of the best beaches in the area with a small sandy plateau nestled on a cliff off the coast. Polhena beach is a popular area for snorkeling and sea turtle fishing. The beach is a popular destination among locals and tourists alike.

Star Fort

Matara Star Fort is a fort located on the eastern bank of the Nilwala River, approximately 350 m from the entrance to Matara Fort, Sri Lanka. Built by the Dutch in 1765, the fort was originally known as "Route One". It is made of granite rock and coral.

Veherahena Temple

Veherahena is a popular temple among Buddhist pilgrims.It located on the Matara-Kekanadura road via Weherahena about 6 km from Matara town. It is to be the first and the largest tunnel temple in the world. The temple houses the highest Buddha state in Sri Lanka which is 88 cubits in height.

Paraviduwa Temple

This is located on a small island. Matara Paravai Duwa Viharaya is a peaceful and scenic Buddhist temple. Surrounded by the ocean, the Matara Pigeon Temple is home to Buddhist monks and is ideal for soul searching. We urge you to always maintain silence and sophistication during your visit

Matara Beach

Matara Beach is a beautiful beach located in Kotuwegoda, Matara. It is a great place to relax with the sea breeze and waves. Many locals roam the coast. An "island temple" also known as the "Pigeon Dua Temple" is located here. It can also be accessed by a sea bridge.

Dondra Head Lighthouse

Dondra Head Lighthouse is a lighthouse located at Dondra Point in the south of Sri Lanka, the tallest lighthouse in Sri Lanka and one of the tallest lighthouses in Southeast Asia. The Dondra Lighthouse is operated and maintained by the Sri Lanka Ports Authority. This lighthouse was built in 1890. In this tower The height is 49 meters. These The lighthouse is located near the village of Dondra and is approximately 6 km southeast of Matara

Crow Island

Crow Island is located across the Polhena coast where the Nilwala River meets the Indian Ocean. Lush with lush greenery and deserts, walking trails, mangroves and freshwater lakes, the island is of great aesthetic value. The island hosts a mix of ecosystems. Crow Island is not suitable for swimming or bathing as the sea is rough.

Mirissa Cocunut Hill

Coconut Tree Mountain is a private coconut grove with a temple located on a high hill overlooking the glistening waters of the Indian Ocean and the golden shores. The mountains near the city can be accessed day or night at no cost. You will be amazed by the most breathtaking view of Mirissa and its golden beaches. The islands are the perfect place to watch the sunset.

Old Dutch Trade Center

Now considered as a market was once a trade centre for the Dutch people. This place is in the shape of the English alphabet 'T'. it is located on the western part of the town, back in the time the market was bloomed with various trade activities. The town is composed of high and imposing roofs.
The structure is held in place by the wooden beams and columns, while the welcoming is done by the majestic entrance. The roof is full of clay tiles and it is locally called as Peti Ulu.
