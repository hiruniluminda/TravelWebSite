WELCOME TO POLONNARUWA DISTRICT

The total land area of Polonnaruwa district is 3,403 sq. Km. The total population was 358,984.
We welcome you to find your unique Polonnaruwa district travel experience.

Rankoth Vehera

Rankoth Vehera is an ancient stupa located in Sri Lanka. Rankoth Vehera is built in the tradition of the Anuradhapura Maha Vihara Stupa and shows many features close to the Ruwanweliseya in Anuradhapura. In the inscription near the stupa, the stupa is referred to as "Ruwanweli" but later became known as Rankoth Vehera today.

Polonnaruwa Vatadage

It is believed that this was built during the reign of King Parakramabahu I, to house the sacred tooth relics of the Supreme Buddha or during the reign of King Nissanka Malla to place the relics of the Supreme Buddha. At that time these sacred relics were symbols of statehood. Located in the historic city of Polonnaruwa, this is the finest Vatadage in the country. Excavations at the Polonnaruwa Vatadage began in 1903 after centuries of decay.

Nissanka Latha Mandapaya

According to an inscription here, this is the place where King Nissanka Malla heard pirith. There is evidence that the Nissanka Latha Mandapa was covered with a roof at that time. Similar stone carvings can be seen in the building adjacent to the seven-story palace.

Pothgul Viharaya

The Potgul Vihara is a circular building on a high platform near the statue known as the Potgul Vehera Statue, the Parakramabahu Statue and the Pulasti Statue that we find in the Polonnaruwa Kingdom. Built entirely in the tradition of brick and mortar, this is a two storied building surrounded by a circular wall and the Potgul Vehera is located on its highest floor. It has an ornate stone doorway and four small stupas at the four corners of the courtyard.

Manampitiya Bridge

Manampitiya Bridge is the second longest bridge in Sri Lanka and is 302 meters long. Here you can see two bridges, the old iron bridge is used for the railway and the new bridge is used for the highway. Formed across the Mahaweli River connecting the North Central and Eastern Provinces

Parakrama Samudraya

Parakrama Samudraya, built by King Parakramabahu the Great, is located in Polonnaruwa. The present tank bund is 12.38 km long and 9.45 m high. The stream area of the reservoir is 71.71 sq. Km and the spread is 2539.5 ha. The water capacity of the reservoir is 134.07 million cubic meters

Gal Vihara

Gal Viharaya is an ancient statue house. However, due to the large number of stone Buddha statues that could be seen here, the name 'Uttararama Pilimageya Gal Viharaya' has become popular among the people. It is said that this was built by the great Parakumbavas.

Lankatilaka Temple

Lankathilaka is said to be the largest temple among the temples built by King Parakramabahu. Its roof is made of bricks and has a circular shape. The Prathima house consists of three parts, the nave, the antarala, and the oral pavilion, and the standing Buddha statue is about 42 feet high. The hands and face of the Buddha statue have been destroyed.
This Buddha statue is made to represent the Abhaya seal on the right and the robe on the left. The Buddha statue sits on a 4 feet high brick pedestal. Below the end of the Buddha 's robe is a small lion statue. Its staircase is still visible, and one has to climb to the top of the statue without crossing the body. It can be mentioned as another unique feature that exists here.

Giritale Wewa

Giritale Lake is a large lake built by King Agbo II.
