WELCOME TO BATTICALOA DISTRICT

The total land area of the district is 2,854 sq km and the total population is 525,142.
We welcome you to find your unique Batticaloa district travel experience.

Batticaloa Fort

Batticaloa Fort is located amidst lush greenery and tidal ponds. It was first built in 1628 by the Portuguese. Its attractive solid walls, which retain its excellence and aesthetic dignity, are stunning. The panoramic view of the quiet tidal pool makes the place even more beautiful.

Kallady Beach

There is an abandoned section of Kalladi beach and the surrounding tsunami evidence is very well preserved. Kalladi Beach is an amazing beach on the east coast of Sri Lanka. A perfect place to relax your evening.

Kalkudah and Passikudah

North of Batticaloa, on both sides of the palm-fringed Kalkudah precipice, are two magnificent beaches, Kalkudah and Pasikudah. Twin Bay beaches, surrounded by monsoon cliffs, are the perfect place for wind surfing, bathing, sailing and more. It is one of the most notable known rock formations on the east coast of Sri Lanka.

Batticaloa Lighthouse

This historic lighthouse is not open, but you can take a boat ride around it to get an incredible view of the lighthouse.

Kallady Bridge

During the period from April to September, along the Kalladi Bridge, a clear but subtle melody sounds like a guitar snatched from the lagoon water. The obscure music is attributed to some kind of sound emanating from the marine life found in the lagoon. People immerse the end of a spade in water and grab the opposite end of their ear to match the music clearly.
