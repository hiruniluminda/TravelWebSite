WELCOME TO KEGALLE DISTRICT

It covers an area of 1663 square kilometers and has a population of 785,524 according to the 2001 census.
We welcome you to find your unique Kegalle district travel experience.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

It was set up to take care of orphaned baby elephants and injured elephants. It is currently home to 93 elephants. At that time Pinnawala was a very difficult area with lush coconut plantations and most importantly there was no shortage of ivory. Also, the huge amount of water that the elephants need is supplemented by the presence of the “Maoya” that flows nearby.

Belilena Cave

Belilena is a famous large cave in Sri Lanka located at a distance of 8 km from Kitulgala town. Evidence of prehistoric human existence 32,000 years ago was recorded at this site. The bones of ten people were found.

Algama Water Fall

It is not a famous waterfall, it is a hidden beauty in this paradise. Surrounded by that beautiful environment in the Algama area of the Kegalle District. The waterfall is about 6 meters. You can easily climb up the cliffs and find several pools where you can bathe and swim.

Bible Rock

Bible Rock, also known as Batalegala in Sinhala. The mountain gets its name because its surface is very similar to a book / Bible. Bible Rock is clearly visible to anyone, especially those traveling by train from Colombo to Kandy. This is a popular mountaineering destination. It is approximately 797.7 meters high and 710 meters wide. The top of the rock consists of an abandoned temple with a small stupa and a temple room.
