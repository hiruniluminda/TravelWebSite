WELCOME TO KANDY DISTRICT

Kandy District is a district in the Central Province, India. The total land area is 1906.3 square kilometers. The district capital is Kandy
We welcome you to find your unique Kandy district travel experience.

Temple Of The Sacred Tooth Relic

Known as the Temple of the Tooth, it is considered to be the most sacred Buddhist temple in the world. The Tooth Relic of the Buddha is kept in a golden chamber.The Temple of the Tooth is decorated with silver, ivory and brass carvings.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

The Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage was established to provide shelter and nutrition for abandoned, injured and disabled elephants. There were 84 elephants here in 2008. It is also spread over an area of about 25 acres.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains is one of the most beautiful places in Sri Lanka that attracts the attention of locals and foreigners alike. An invaluable global treasure, it is a National Park that is unique in its biodiversity as well as its archaeological value. Anyone searching for the end of the world will find a beautiful plain with the most isolation found in any other ecosystem in our country. It also has a land area of 3160 hectares.
There are about 101 species of plants here. 49 of them are endemic to Sri Lanka. What is special is that most of them are unique to the Horton Plains. The plant density in the Horton Plains is 2.861 plants per hectare. Currently, 98 species of birds, 14 species of mammals, 16-20 species of amphibians and 40 species of butterflies have been identified.

Peradeniya Botanical Garden

The Royal Botanic Gardens is located in Peradeniya, Sri Lanka. It is considered to be one of the five Botanic Gardens in Sri Lanka and receives an annual rainfall of 200 out of 365 days of the year.

Bahiravokanda Vihara Buddha Statue

Bhairavakanda Kanda Temple, also known as the Sri Maha Bodhi Temple, is best known for its giant Buddha statue, which can be seen from anywhere in Kandy. This statue shows the Supreme Buddha sitting in the Nirvana posture, the place where he first attained enlightenment. Reaching a height of over 25 meters, it is one of the tallest Buddha statues in Sri Lanka.

Commonwealth War Cemetery

Kandy War Cemetery, formerly known as Pitakanda Army Cemetery, is a British Army Cemetery located in Kandy, Sri Lanka. There are 107 British, 35 East African, 26 Sri Lankan, 23 Indian, 6 Canadian, 3 Italian, 1 French and 2 unidentified persons. , 16 Navy, 2 Unidentified, 1 Merchant Navy and 1 National Fire Brigade
