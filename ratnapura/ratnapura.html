WELCOME TO RATNAPURA DISTRICT

Ratnapura is the main center of the gem industry in Sri Lanka .It is a major crossroads between the southern plains and the eastern hills. It is a district that serves many of the surrounding towns.
We welcome you to find your unique Ratnapura district travel experience.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a national park covering an area of ​​11,250 hectares in the Rakwana Range 300-1170 m high in the southwestern part of the Central Highlands of Sri Lanka, bordering the Galle District. It is a World Heritage Site and a World Heritage Site.

Bopathella Falls

This waterfall is called Bopath Ella as it is very similar in shape to a Bo leaf. This waterfall is known as one of the most popular tourist attractions in Sri Lanka

Kitulgala

Kitulgala is a town located west of the road from Kandy to Nuwara Eliya and is famous for its adventure sports. It is a city in the middle of the jungle. Most of the adventure sports come from Colombo. The main attraction of Kitulgala is the white water rafting that can drive the tides of the Kelani River. Other than that it is popular for sports like confidence jumping, stream sliding, waterfall abseiling, jungle trekking

Maha Saman Devalaya

Maha Saman Devalaya, also known as Samanthakuta, which means the mountain of the god Saman, is a shrine located in Ratnapura, which is considered to be the abode of Sri Pada Kanda, the place where the Buddha often placed his left foot emblem when he visited Sri Lanka.

Bambarakanda Waterfalls

Bambarakanda Falls is the tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka. Its height is 263 m. It is the 299th tallest waterfall in the world. It is formed by a small stream which is a tributary of the Walawe river. A forest full of pine trees is found at Bambarakanda Falls. Stairs are designed with safety fences for easy access. The short distance you have to walk through the pine forest is quite impressive.

Gem Mines

Ratnapura is famous for its gems. A large number of gem mines are found here.
