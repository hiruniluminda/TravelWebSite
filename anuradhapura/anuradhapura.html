WELCOME TO ANURADHAPURA DISTRICT

Anuradhapura District is a district in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka. The total land area of the district is 7,179 sq. Km. The total population in 2001 was 745,693.
We welcome you to find your unique Anuradhapura district travel experience.

Sri Maha Bodiya

It is believed that this tree sprouted from a cut brought by Buddhagaya in India where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment. Today the tree is surrounded by statues, canals and gold fences, which add to the sanctity and spirituality of the site and enhance its natural beauty. Although it is a sacred tree to Buddhists, many people of other religions also visit this tree to enjoy the serenity of their daily lives immersed in its serene beauty.Another notable feature is the Prarthana Bodhi. Many devotees believe that this tree has the power to fulfill the desires of everyone who comes to the complex.

Lovamahapaya

Lovamahapaya is a 2000 year old palace built by King Dutugemunu. And nine floors up to 150 feet high, with 400 rooms and 1,600 stone pillars 400 feet long on either side. It is also known as Brazen Place because of the bronze tiles used on its roof. Lowamahapaya was built as a residence for chanting "Pirith" on Poya days. It can also accommodate up to 1000 monks. The Maha Vihara Ashram also has a 45 foot rice boat carved out of stone that can prepare alms for 3000 monks at a time.

Thuparamaya

Thuparamaya is considered to be the first stupa built in Sri Lanka after the introduction of Buddhism. The name Thuparamaya is derived from the combination of the words stupa and aramaya. This place was a monastery where monks lived.

Ruwanweliseya

Ruwanweli Seya is a stupa that has been revered by Buddhists all over the world for its architectural features and majesty. The stupa is one of the sixteenth and eighth sites. At 300 feet high and 950 feet in diameter, it is one of the tallest monuments in the world today.

Jetavanaramaya Monastery

A consumer chaitya. At 400 feet, it is the tallest stupa in the world. It is also the largest brick-built building to date.

Samadhi Buddha Statue

The Samadhi Buddha statue is located in the Mahamevna Garden in Anuradhapura, Sri Lanka. This statue depicts Lord Buddha meditating while sitting on a meditation seal. In this meditative posture, the Buddha sits with his hands on his hips, with his arms folded and his hands turned upside down. The Anuradhapura Samadhi Statue is 7'3 "high and is made of a single granite stone.

Isurumuniya

The Isurumuni Temple was built by King Devanampiyatissa who ruled in the ancient city of Anuradhapura in ancient Sri Lanka. There is a temple connected to a cave, and above it is a rock. There is a small stupa. It seems that the stupa was built today.
At the bottom are carved ivory figures depicting a pond rising on either side of an opening. An image of a horse is also carved on the rock. The carving of the Isurumuni couple carved on a stone slab may have been carved elsewhere and later fixed at that place. There is a wedding garden near the temple

Abhayagiri Viharaya

This stupa was built by King Walagamba. It is believed that the stupa was built at a place where the Buddha's feet were placed after he ascended to Cyril.

Mihintale

This is a 1000 m high remnant mountain. Also known as Chethiyagiriya or Sagiri, it is more popularly known as Mihintale. Arahant Mahinda, who came from India, met King Devanampiyatissa and his entourage on Poson Poya Day and preached Buddhism in Mihintale.

Ritigala

It has a climate close to the wet zone climate pattern. There are delicate springs that flow continuously throughout the year. Historic ruins are important as a city. There are many species of plants in this forest. Birds include a variety of butterflies as well as four-limbed animals such as elephants, tigers, bears, deer, deer, pythons, snakes, snakes and reptiles

Ratnaprasada

Turning left at the site of the large stone boat, you can see the Ratnaprasada among the ruins. This is a monastery. The building is built on a stone platform.

Ranmasu Uyana

Ranmasu Uyana is spread over an area of about 40 acres. Lakdiva is considered to be a unique creation that showcases ancient horticultural art and subtle water management techniques. Built using rock and stone slabs, the beautiful ponds, baths and all the features of a garden can be seen here. The brick canals that carry water to the ponds in the garden still survive today.
