WELCOME TO TRINCOMALEE DISTRICT

The total land area of Trincomalee District is 2,727 sq. Km. The total population was 378,182.
We welcome you to find your unique Trincomalee district travel experience.

Koneswaram Temple

Trincomalee is one of the first places to be removed from the tourist map. This important Hindu place of worship is believed to be the one with the Pancha Ishwaram Swayambhu Linga. This shrine exudes sacred vibrations and is a wonderful place to seek solace in the divine presence. Surrounded by breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean, this magnificent temple is very beautiful.

Fort Fredrick

The presence of free-roaming spotted deer adds to the charm of passing through this fort. Fort Frederick, built by the Portuguese, is partially under military occupation and houses a famous Buddhist temple and a sacred Hindu temple.

Velgama Vehera

Walking through the ruins of this ancient Buddhist temple built by King Devanamiyatissa against a beautiful backdrop amidst tall trees and grass is a calming experience. The original crystal Buddha statue is still standing and the new monastery offers an opportunity to meditate in this holy place.

Nilaveli Beach

This relaxing paradise is perfect for swimming, whale watching, volleyball or relaxing by the sea. Further north, Nilaveli beach has been completely polluted by a large, calm and tourist attack.

Pigeon Island National Park

Consisting of two small islands, this national park is home to endangered rock pigeons, exotic marine life and fascinating rock formations. The dusty white sands, glistening rock pools and shallow turquoise waters make this an unforgettable trip.
