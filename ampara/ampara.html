WELCOME TO AMPARA DISTRICT

Ampara District is a district in the Eastern Province of Sri Lanka. The total land area of the district is 4,415 sq km and the total population is 648,057.
We welcome you to find your unique Ampara district travel experience.

Kumana National Park

Spread over an area of 18,149 hectares, Kumana National Park is a well-known eco-tourist attraction and bird sanctuary where a large number of birds breed and roam. Kumana Villu is an important part of the park, a 200 hectare natural marsh lake that feeds on the Kumbukkan Oya through a narrow half mile long canal.
A hook with a very rare black neck is also found in the swamp. Yala National Park is also home to mammals such as elephants and leopards. The vegetation of Kumana area consists mainly of mangroves, Kumbuk trees and Karan ferns as well as open swamps.

Gal Oya National Park

Galoya National Park serves as the main catchment area of ​​the Senanayake Sea, the largest reservoir in Sri Lanka. An important feature of Galoya National Park is the herd of elephants that can be seen throughout the year. Three important herbs in Ayurvedic medicine are: Terminalia chebula, Terminalia bellirica and Emblica officinalis.

Deegavapi Stupa

Deeghawapiya is popularly known as one of the sixteen sacred places where the Supreme Buddha visited Sri Lanka. According to the Mahavamsa, King Saddhatissa built the Deeghawapiya Buddhist Temple. Legend has it that this great stupa was built by burying the 'nail relics' of the Supreme Buddha.

Lahugala Magul Maha Viharaya

Magul Maha Viharaya is located on the northern border of Lahugala National Park, about 22 km from Siyambalanduwa town and about 11 km from Pottuvil town. Lahugala was part of the Ruhuna Kingdom in ancient Sri Lanka. The ruins of the Magul Maha Vihara are a major tourist attraction in the Eastern Province. This temple is also an archeological protected monument in the country.

Crocodile Rock

Although this is a small rock, the climb is difficult. There is clear water where you can enjoy amazing sunsets as well as surfing. Next to the rock is a forest where crocodiles live in large numbers, so the rock is called crocodile rock because they also come to this area. There are warning signs in areas where there are crocodiles.

Aranthalawa Massacre Site

The Aranthalawa Massacre, also known as the Aranthalawa Monk Massacre, is a massacre carried out by the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam on June 2, 1987 at Aranthalawa in the Ampara District. The killing of 33 Buddhist monks and four civilians. The massacre is considered to be the most notorious and destructive act committed by the LTTE during the Civil War and a replica of this incident has been created in the area.
