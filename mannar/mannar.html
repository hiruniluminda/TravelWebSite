WELCOME TO MANNAR DISTRICT

The total land area of the district is 1,996 sq km and the total population is 99,051. The easiest and shortest sea route from Shangri-Lanka to the Indian subcontinent is across the island of Mannar.
We welcome you to find your unique Mannar district travel experience.

Mannar Island

Mannar Island is famous for its sunny weather and ancient biobab trees. It was once the center of the island's prosperous pearl industry. Visitors to Mannar can also visit Kiri Beach, the Talaimannar Lighthouse, the 700 year old Baobab Tree and the Mannar Fort.The island covers an area of about 125 square kilometers, mostly white sand and palm trees. There are also palm tree forests near the coast inhabited by several tribes of monkeys.

Mannar Fort

The square fort was built by the Portuguese in the middle of the 16th century but was captured by the Dutch in 1658. Once the Dutch took possession of it, they made no changes to the building except to fortify it with further attacks. In 1795, the Dutch surrendered to the British and the fort was later captured by British troops.
The forts reach a height of about 3.5 m, the solid walls are about 1.5 m, and the forts are approximately 14.5 m long. Inside the walls of the fort are the ruins of what looks like a church, as well as two watchtowers at its corners.

Baobab Tree

It is said to have been planted by Arab traders who came to the island centuries ago. This baobab tree is 700 years old.

Mannar Bird's Santuary

Several rare birds can be seen here, including spotted-billed ducks, comb ducks, long-fingered hawks, peregrine falcons and the very rare eastern black-tailed Godwit. Visitors can also see flamingos on this site. Nearly 150 species of birds are reported to be found in the sanctuary, and the marine ecosystem of the site is home to more than 60 species, including fish, turtles, dugongs and crocodiles.

Adam's Bridge

Adam's Bridge is a natural limestone chain between Pamban Island, also known as Rameshwaram Island, southeast of Tamil Nadu and Mannar, India. According to geological evidence, this bridge was an ancient land connection between India and Sri Lanka.
