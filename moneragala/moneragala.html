WELCOME TO MONERAGALA DISTRICT

The total land area of the district is 5,636 sq km and the total population is 448,194. This district is the second largest district in Sri Lanka.
We welcome you to find your unique Moneragala district travel experience.

Biso Pokuna

There are ruins of a palace complex in the village of Galabedde, 10.5 km from Monaragala on the Pottuvil road. These include impressive sluice gates, ponds, moats, ramparts, and palace ruins. The pool in Maliga Garden is one of the finest works of ancient Sinhala architecture. The pool is made up of solid rocks with four water entrances on four sides.

Udawalawa

Due to the different habitats in the Udawalawe area, it is home to animals of different spectra. However, the main attraction of Udawalawe is the elephants and a herd of elephants are believed to be the residents of the park. Udawalawe National Park is home to a number of Sri Lankan wildlife species, including a wide variety of birds, some of which are endemic to fish, reptiles and amphibians.
About 600 to 700 Sri Lankan elephants live within the boundaries of this wildlife sanctuary, making it an amazing and memorable experience to see these elephants in their natural habitat, both adult and young.

Okkampitiya

Another beautiful area located in the Monaragala District. The village life in Okkampitiya is breathtaking. You have to go to that area to see it and experience that culture.

Buduruwagala Buddhist Temple

Buddha statue at Buduruwagala temple These sculptures, which are carved slightly out of the rock, are considered to be Mahayana sculptures. The main Buddha statue is about 51 feet high and has six statues, three on each side. To the right of the Buddha statue are the statues of Avalokiteshvara Natha, Bodhisattva, Goddess Tara and Sudhana. There is also a picture of Maitreya Bodhisattva on the left and a Vajrapani Bodhisattva on the other side, another image which is difficult to identify.In addition, there are many buildings and ruins belonging to the Buduruwagala Sangarama scattered all over the land.

Senanayaka Samudraya

Senanayake Sea is the largest reservoir in Sri Lanka and the man-made lake. It is spread over an area of 950000000 square meters.

Galoya National Park

Galoya National Park is a natural evolution that has not been touched by humans for centuries. Senanayake Samudrawewa, the largest inland body of water in Sri Lanka, is surrounded by over 25,000 hectares of evergreen forests and open habitats. Galoya National Park is the only boat safari in Sri Lanka. It is home to 32 mammal species, including langurs, talk macaws, leopards, lazy bears, wild boars, buffaloes and deer. In addition, there are nearly 150 different bird species, making it one of the best places in the world to see an Asian elephant.
