WELCOME TO HAMBANTOTA DISTRICT

Hambantota District is a district in Southern Province, Sri Lanka. It is one of 25 districts of Sri Lanka, the second level administrative division of the country. Hambantota District is located on the southeastern coast of Sri Lanka. It has an area of 2,593 kmÂ² and a very dry climate. The district capital is Hambantota town.Other prominent towns include Tangalle, Ambalantota, Tissamaharama, and Beliatta. The area has traditionally been home to Sinhalese and Sri Lankan Malay people, who collectively make up 98% of the district's population. The long history of Malay settlement in the district has impacted the local culture of Hambantota, with Sri Lankan Malay being a shared language between the Sri Lankan Malays and some members of the Sinhalese community. We welcome you to find your unique Hambantota district travel experience.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is spread over the Hambantota District in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka and the Moneragala District in the Uva Province. Yala National Park is the second largest national park in Sri Lanka. Although Yala covers an area of 1260 square kilometers, only one fifth of that area is open to tourists. The rest of the area is designated as a High Nature Reserve. The rocky outcrop of the arid zone of Sri Lanka, with its thorny bushes and shrubbery, enhances the unique charm of Yala National Park along the southern coast. If we talk about the distribution and diversity of wildlife in Sri Lanka, Yala National Park has the highest wildlife diversity compared to all other wildlife parks. Cockroaches and lagoons of various colors are a common sight at any time of the day. Also, crocodiles are more common around lagoons. As you walk through the dry trees, monkeys and parrots will be able to catch a glimpse of peacocks dancing among the yellow and red dry leaves that fall to the ground, displaying their blue and green feathers. From time to time wild elephants and shrubs and bushes appear across the road and re-enter the forest, bringing invisible tigers to the park. How to reach Yala National Park:- The entrance to the Yala National Park is located in Tissamaharama. The Palatupana entrance can be reached by driving about 12 km from Kirinda. The total distance from Colombo to the Palatupana entrance is about 305 km.

Ussangoda National Park

Ussangoda is located near the Nonagama beach in the southern Ambalantota region of Sri Lanka. If you are traveling towards Kataragama on the Colombo-Kataragama Pannaradhana road, you can reach this area by turning right before Nonagama Junction and paying about 2 km towards the sea. Based on a number of mysterious and intriguing folklores, this place is a beautiful place to behold. The scenic beauty of the plateau is further enhanced by the plateau overlooking the plains.

Ridiyagama Safari Park

According to the new zoo concept, the first safari park established in Sri Lanka where animals can be seen roaming freely is located in the Ridiyagama area near Ambalantota in the Hambantota District. This park can be reached by going about 17 km from Ambalantota town on Ridiyagama road or turning off at Padalangala junction on Embilipitiya Nonagama road and taking Suriyawewa road and turning at Viharagala junction for about 4 km. Currently, it is home to about 200 species of animals. The park is equipped with air-conditioned safety buses that carry about 20 visitors at a time. In addition, a seven-passenger vehicle can be purchased for an additional fee if required.

Mirijjawila Botanic Garden

Mirijjawila Botanic Gardens is located on the Colombo-Kataragama main road. The Mattala International Airport is to the left of the Botanic Gardens and the Magampura Port is to the right. The park consists of three lakes namely Kohombagas Wewa, Malithangas Wewa and Dematatta Wewa. These tanks are designed to retain moisture and coolness within the garden premises.

Walawe River Safari

The popular Walawa River boat ride will take you through an area rich in biodiversity consisting of six species of mangroves, 52 species of fish, 72 species of birds, 38 species of plants and 28 species of animals. There are trees near the river where bats and cranes take turns staying. Bats move there when large black buckets and cranes hang from the edges

Mulkirigala Rock Temple

Mulkirigala Rajamaha Viharaya is an ancient temple of archeological value located in the Hambantota District of the Southern Province of Sri Lanka. This temple is located in Weeraketiya area and it is 2 km from Mulkirigala junction. Located at a distance of about. This temple can be reached from Dickwella or Tangalle areas and the Mulkirigala temple is also known as Kuda Sigiriya. Mulkirigala Temple is located on a 205 m high natural rock and is surrounded by four other rocks called Benagala, Kondagala, Bodhagolla and Seelawathugala. The Mulkirigala Vihara consists of five main courtyards namely Siyambala Maluwa, Pahala Maluwa, Meda Maluwa, Rajamaha Maluwa and Udaha Maluwa and to reach the top of the temple one has to climb about 550 steps. The upper courtyard consists of a stupa, a twenty-fruited Ruha Bodhi, a bell tower, a garrison and a pond.The courtyard consists of a stupa, a bodhi tree, a courtyard, a cave temple, and a monastery in the middle. The office associated with the temple is located in the lower courtyard and also houses the stupa, the Bo tree, the Rajamaha Nalen temple, the Paduma Rahath temple, the inscription, the old boundary stone, the Bhanaka cave and the rock inscription. The Siyambala courtyard houses the museum, the temple and the rest house.
