WELCOME TO NUWARA ELIYA DISTRICT

Nuwara Eliya is a hill station in the Central Province of Sri Lanka. The city is the administrative capital of the Nuwara Eliya District with its beautiful landscape and temperate climate. It is located at an altitude of 1,868 m and is considered to be the most important place for tea production in Sri Lanka. Nuwara Eliya is famous for its temperate and cool climate. The coolest place in Sri Lanka.
We welcome you to find your unique Nuwara Eliya district travel experience.

Victoria Park

Victoria Park Located in the center of Nuwara Eliya town. It has a lot of colorful flowers and is a great place to have fun and relax. It also includes a play area for children and a train ride for adults. The place is famous for bird watching and has a large variety of plants and trees..

Gregory Lake

Also known as Lake Gregory Lake or Gregory Reservoir, it is located in the heart of the mountain town in the tea country of Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka. Lake Gregory was built in 1873 during the reign of Sir William Gregory, Governor of the United Kingdom.

Ambewela Farm

Ambewela is a village and a hill station in the Nuwara Eliya District of Sri Lanka. This area is also sometimes referred to as "Little New Zealand". The city is located approximately 17 km (11 mi) southeast of the Nuwara Eliya District Capital.

Moon Plains

This is one of the best places in Sri Lanka to have a panoramic view of the mountains. A recently opened attraction in Nuwara Eliya with a different kind of experience for tourists. The main attraction of Moon Plains is the Mini Worlds End, which offers a 360 degree view of the surrounding mountains and cities. It is a wide open area where wild boars, cows, buffaloes and deer can be seen and 105 species of birds have been recorded in the area which is endemic to Sri Lanka. At the scenic spot, you can see the top of 9 mountains in Sri Lanka on a clear day.

Hakgala Botanical Garden

Hakgala Botanic Gardens is the second largest of the five botanical gardens in Sri Lanka and is adjacent to the Hakgala Strict Nature Reserve, home to a variety of wildlife including bears, monkeys and birds. The road to Hakgala on the Badulla road is mostly a pleasant hillside through the pine forests. The cozy cool weather that prevails throughout the year creates a real paradise. It has thousands of plant species.
The main parts of the garden are the rock garden, the water garden, the fern garden, the Japanese garden and the seed room. The dreaded Hakgala rock provides a stunning backdrop to the park. It rises 450 meters.

Ramboda falls

Ramboda Falls is located in the Pussellawa area in the Nuwara Eliya District of the Central Province of Sri Lanka. This is a 109 meter high waterfall. In terms of height, it ranks 11th in Sri Lanka and 729th in the world.

Laxapana Falls

Laxapana Falls is located in the town of Maskeliya in the Nuwara Eliya District. At a height of 126 meters, it is the 8th tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka and the 625th tallest waterfall in the world. It is named after two hydropower plants, the old Laxapana power plant and the new Laxapana power plant. Laxapana Falls is formed by the Maskeliya Oya, a tributary of the Kelani River.

Piduruthalagala

Mount Pidurutalagala is the highest peak in Sri Lanka. The height is 2524 meters. In addition to being the highest peak in Sri Lanka, this place is also important as a place where four maps are sent out of the 92 maps that divide Sri Lanka

Seetha Amman Kovil

Seetha Amman Kovil is located in the Seetha Eliya area, about 1 km from the Hakgala Botanic Gardens. According to the legend of Ramayana, this temple is believed to have been located at the place where King Ravana of the Kingdom of Ceylon is said to have abducted the Indian goddess Seetha.
