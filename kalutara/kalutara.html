WELCOME TO KALUTARA DISTRICT

Kalutara District is a district in the Western Province of Sri Lanka. The total land area of the district is 1,598 square kilometers and the total population is 1,066,239.
We welcome you to find your unique Kalutara district travel experience.

Kalutara Bodiya

The Kalutara Bo tree which is more than 2320 years old belongs to the second branch of the Sri Maha Bodhi tree which is the oldest tree in the world.

Kalutara Beach

Kalutara Beach is a golden beach that stretches from Waskaduwa to Payagala. Divided by the Black River estuary, it has its own charm and some darker shade of sand around the estuary. It is also an opportunity to have fun with cultural and religious attractions.

Thudugala Ella Waterfalls

Has become a well-known bathing place. There is also a natural pool of water surrounded by "kettle" plants and ferns. The nearby reserve is home to many migratory and native birds. Another special feature is that a tunnel runs beneath the waterfall and rises to one side.

Richmond Castle

Richmond Castle is an old Edward mansion in the rural areas of Kalutara, Sri Lanka. Built in the early 1900s, it was the home of the local baron Don Arthur de Silva Wijesinghe Siriwardena.

Asokaramaya Buddhist Temple

Ashokaramaya Buddhist Temple is a Buddhist temple located in Kalutara, Sri Lanka. It was built in the 1870s to accommodate the disciples of the Aluthgama Sangha who came to hear the sermon of the Bhikkhu.

Holy Cross Church

The Holy Cross Church is located on the main road in the south of Kalutara and is prominent in the city center. The church is located on a high ground and faces the ocean, offering some great coastal views.
